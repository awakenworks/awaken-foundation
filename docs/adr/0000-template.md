# ADR-0000: Title

- **Status:** Proposed | Accepted | Superseded by [ADR-XXXX](./XXXX-....md) | Deprecated
- **Implementation:** planned | in-progress | done | n/a
- **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD

> **Status** is the *decision* state. `Proposed` is freely editable; once
> `Accepted` the record is append-mostly (fix typos, never rewrite the
> decision — supersede with a new ADR instead). `Superseded` and `Deprecated`
> are terminal.
>
> **Implementation** is an independent review signal — *not* part of the
> decision. It may change freely as code lands, is exempt from the
> append-mostly rule, and never gates `Status`. Use `n/a` for decisions with no
> code (process, conventions). Validated by `scripts/ci/check_adr.py`.

## Context

What forces are at play? What problem are we solving, and what constraints
bound the solution? State the facts, not the decision.

## Decision

The decision, in active voice: "We will ...". Be specific enough that a reader
can tell whether a future change violates it.

## Consequences

What becomes easier, and what becomes harder. Note any new guardrail this
decision introduces and where its mechanical enforcer lives.

## Alternatives considered

The options not taken, and why.
